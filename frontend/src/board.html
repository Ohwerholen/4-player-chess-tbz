1. Board Setup: The board is 12x12, with each player’s pieces in their corner.

2. Players and Turns: Four players (Red, Blue, Yellow, Green) take turns clockwise, starting from Red.

3. Objective: Checkmate all opponents. There are no teams; every player competes solo.

4. Capturing and Eliminations: Checkmated players’ pieces freeze but remain on the board and can be captured.

5. Check and Checkmate: Checkmated players are eliminated. Stalemates also eliminate players, but their pieces freeze.

6. Pawn Promotion: Pawns promote upon reaching the 8th square, excluding promotion to a king.

7. Checkmate Variations: If multiple players checkmate a king, the last player to check gets points.

8. End of Game: The game ends when only one player remains or three players are eliminated.

9. Middle Pawn: Center pawns choose left or right for their first move, then continue in that direction.
